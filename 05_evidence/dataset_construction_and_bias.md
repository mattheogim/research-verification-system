## Dataset Construction

### Dataset A (17 sources)
**Origin:** Evidence table from research-verification-system's own architecture document (v0.4.1 §12-13)
**How selected:** NOT randomly sampled. These are the papers/sources that an LLM (ChatGPT) generated as supporting and counter-evidence for the research verification framework itself.
**Selection bias:** High — these were chosen by an LLM to support a specific argument. Error patterns may reflect this LLM's tendencies, not LLM citation errors in general.
**Domain:** Mixed (systematic review methodology, automation bias, research bureaucracy, citation verification tools)
**Identifier coverage:** 5 arXiv, 4 DOI-only, 3 non-papers (PyPI, API, platform), 1 web article, 4 no identifier

### Dataset B (42 papers)
**Origin:** Paper catalog from meta-architect project (`research/papers-catalog.md` → `papers.yaml`)
**How selected:** NOT randomly sampled. These are the papers an LLM (ChatGPT) curated to support a multi-agent architecture design project.
**Selection bias:** High — same LLM (ChatGPT) generated both A and B catalogs, same human operator (Matteo Kim). Error patterns may be correlated.
**Domain:** CS/AI (multi-agent systems, LLM debate, blackboard architecture, scaling laws)
**Identifier coverage:** 35 arXiv, 2 DOI-only, 5 no identifier

### Dataset C-1 (20 papers)
**Origin:** LLM-generated from scratch ("list 20 federated learning papers 2024-2025")
**How selected:** Prompt-generated, no human curation or review
**Selection bias:** Different bias — pure LLM generation without human filtering. 100% error rate reflects unchecked generation.
**Domain:** CS (federated learning)

### Dataset C-2 (15 papers)
**Origin:** Public GitHub awesome-list (human-curated)
**How selected:** Taken from an existing community-maintained list
**Selection bias:** Low — human-curated by multiple contributors, not LLM-generated
**Domain:** CS/AI

### Dataset C-3 (10 papers)
**Origin:** PubMed medical papers
**How selected:** LLM-generated from PubMed queries
**Selection bias:** Domain shift test — intentionally non-CS to test generalizability
**Domain:** Medical/biomedical

## Known Biases

### 1. Ecosystem correlation (A + B)
Datasets A and B come from the same person's projects, likely generated by the same LLM (ChatGPT) in similar time periods. Error patterns may be correlated rather than independent. **Mitigation:** Dataset C provides independent samples.

### 2. CS/AI domain overrepresentation
73 of 98 papers (74%) are CS/AI. Only 10 are medical (C-3). Results may not generalize to humanities, law, or social sciences where arXiv is not used. **Mitigation:** C-3 tests non-CS. Limitation acknowledged in paper.

### 3. arXiv dependency
Level 1 verification is heavily arXiv-based. Papers without arXiv IDs (23% of A, 12% of B) are harder to verify. verify_papers.py cannot check them at all. **Mitigation:** Manual verification covers non-arXiv papers.

### 4. Temporal bias
All verifications done April 2026. Publication metadata changes over time (preprint → conference → journal). Replication in 6 months may yield different venue_drift counts. **Mitigation:** Timestamps recorded in all CSVs.

### 5. Annotator non-independence
Gold standard: 1 human annotator + 2 LLMs (Claude, GPT). LLMs are "separately prompted" not "independent." Both may share pre-training biases. **Mitigation:** Reported as "calibration evidence" not "definitive inter-annotator reliability."

## Sample Selection for Gold Standard (n=20)

**Method:** Stratified purposive sampling from Datasets A + B combined (58 papers).
**Strata:** EXACT (8), PARTIAL (10), FAIL (2) — oversampling error cases to maximize information for precision/recall calculation.
**Bias:** Overrepresentation of errors vs population base rate. Gold standard P/R/F1 reflects detection performance on this enriched sample, not on a random sample.
**Limitation:** 20/58 = 34% coverage. Remaining 38 papers unverified by human.
